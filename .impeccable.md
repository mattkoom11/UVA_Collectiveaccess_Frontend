# Design context — UVA Fashion Archive

## Audience
Researchers, students, museum visitors, and fashion enthusiasts exploring a university historic clothing collection online—often in focused sessions on laptop or tablet.

## Jobs to be done
- Discover and browse garments with clear hierarchy and fast scan-ability.
- Read provenance and stories without eye strain (long sessions on dark UI).
- Trust the interface to feel archival and intentional, not generic.

## Brand voice (three words)
**Restrained, archival, warm** — editorial museum lighting, not startup gloss.

## Constraints
- Next.js 16, Tailwind v4, existing DM Serif Display + Crimson Pro pairing (core identity).
- WCAG-minded contrast; support high-contrast and font-size accessibility controls.
- Prefer transform/opacity for motion; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Differentiation
Dark warm neutrals with a single ivory accent, generous type for body on dark, and no template tropes (gradient text, neon accents, heavy card chrome).
